About Countable

Take action on stories you care about

Countable makes it easier to understand the laws Congress is considering by providing bill summaries in plain English. It also streamlines the process of contacting lawmakers so voters can let their representatives know how to vote on issues that matter to them.

The Problem

Users have difficulty searching for topics that matter to them

Through guerrilla usability testing, I found that users had difficulty searching for relevant issues, leading users to become frustrated and quit.

Final Deliverables

Reduce the friction of finding relevant issues

Keeping in line with Countable’s design, I re-imagined the in-app search and discovery experience so that users can confidently find their most pressing issues, increasing user engagement.

Quickly navigate to relevant categories

New header navigation tab reduces the cognitive load to interpret the news feed, increasing user engagement.

Improved discovery by preloading search terms

Users can quickly search for previously buried issues without enlisting the keyboard.

Better search results page

Robust search results page gives the user crucial query details and takes relevancy into consideration.

The Results

Increase in users finding relevant issues and confidence in discovered information.

Research & Usability Testing

Users want to quickly connect with relevant issues but feel overwhelmed by the noise

In order to understand the pain points that users are dealing with, I dug into the Countable app store reviews, Pew Research Center articles, and studied competitors to find pain point themes.

I performed 5 guerrilla usability tests in Mountain View on users who feel that staying informed on political issues is important to them. My goal was to understand how users currently engage with news and political advocacy apps.

  1. 1. Users who want to stay informed with current events found that discovering relevant issues is hard to do in most news apps

  2. 2. Users with slight familiarity on topical issues utilized search to find articles to help develop an informed opinion without being overwhelmed

How can I design the discovery experience to empower users to find what matters most to them?

Final Screens

Staying informed should be easy, simple, and rewarding

Pain Point 1: Users were unable to filter the feed

Before:

Users were forced to scroll through the feed to find issues they followed. 0/5 users felt confident in locating an article that was personally relevant to them.

After:

Navigation tabs added an efficient way to filter for necessary information, while also providing clarity within the feed tab.

Pain Point 2: Confusing filters led to unclear search results

Before:

Users had to test each filter prior to search leading to frustration and confusing search results.

After:

Search results are now organized into logical groups making the search experience more comfortable.

Pain Point 3: Issues were hidden under the user profile

Before:

Users were unable to locate issues to follow due to their unintuitive location. Only 1/5 users were able to successfully navigate to the issues page.

After:

The enhanced search experience made finding issues a lot easier. 4/5 users navigated to their desired topic using the explore issues feature.

Reflections

Key Takeaways & Learnings

Design for human error

The initial search functionality isn't very forgiving with user typos and spelling errors. Search is very important and a powerful search feature is the difference between engagement and abandonment.

What I wish I tried during usability tests

"We're not testing you, we're testing the usability of the app." I prefaced each usability test with these words ensuring the gracious participant that he or she could not fail. After wrapping up my initial validation tests, I wondered how the word "test" might give way to negative associations. I would have liked to invite users to "grade" the app versus "test" the app.
